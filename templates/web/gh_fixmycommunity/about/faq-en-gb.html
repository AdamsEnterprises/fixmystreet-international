[% loc('Frequently Asked Questions') %]

What is [% site_name %]?
[% site_name %] is a site to help people report to their local authorities, view, or discuss local problems they’ve found, by simply locating them on a map.
How do I get in touch with [% site_name %]?
Here’s our contact page.
What sort of problems should I report with [% site_name %]?
Currently we’re accepting reports about problems including:
  • Potholes on roads and sidewalks
  • Leaking pipes
  • Problems concerning refuse collection
  • Vandalism (of lorry stations, public centres, Football parks, etc.)
  • Street pigs (Street animals and other potential danger)
  • Broken lights poles and cables
  • Faded road markings and traffic signs
  • Unauthorized Structures and abandoned Government Projects
  • Choked Gutter
  • Report Environmental and social issues
  • Dangerous intersections and infrastructure needed to increase street and traffic safety
  • Other dangerous circumstances posing a threat to people in the communities (trees, collapsing balconies/roofs/houses, insecure construction sites, unwholesome goods)
What isn’t [% site_name %] for?
[% site_name %] is not a way of getting in touch with your authorities for all issues – please use [% site_name %] only for problems such as the above listed. You will need to contact your authorities directly for problems such as:
  • Anti-social behaviour
  • Any urgent or emergency problems
How do I use the site?
After entering a location, you’ll see a map of that area. You can view problems already reported in that area, or report new ones of your own simply by clicking on the map to locate the area where the problem is situated or when you click on the locate me, the site will locate you automatically. Put the pin in the map to show exactly where the problem is, and type in a description of the issue. Then send.
How are the problems solved?
They are reported to the relevant authorities by email. The authorities can then resolve the problem the way they normally would. Alternatively, you can discuss the problem on the website with others, and then together lobby with the authorities to fix it, or fix it directly yourselves.
Is it free?
Yes, it is free to use this site. The FixMyStreet Platform is maintained by a registered charity, so if you want to make a contribution, please do.
Can the report allow to be use by the media houses?
Yes, when proper check is done and found credible FM/TV stations can use as news items to seek redress from the authorities and also to inform the general public whiles reference can be made to this platform as proof.
Can I use [% site_name %] on my mobile?
The FixMyCommunity website already work on your mobile phone, adapting to the size of your screen automatically. There is already a FixMyCommunity apps for Android and iPhone. SMS can also be used to report problems in the community.

Practical Questions

I’m from an authority, where do you send the reports?
You can either leave a test report or contact us to find out where reports go at the moment. Also contact us to update the address or addresses we use.
Will I remain anonymous after reporting an issue?
You can register yourself under any name on the website or use the mobile phone application to report issues under a pseudonym. However if you report through SMS, your mobile phone number will be sent to the authorities.
I’m from an authority, can we have a [% site_name %]?
Quite possibly – contact the team to find out.
Do you remove silly or illegal content?
[% site_name %] is not responsible for the content and accuracy of material submitted by its users. We reserve the right to edit or remove any problems or updates which we consider to be inappropriate upon being informed by a user of the site.
Why can’t I zoom out more on the reporting map?
We want to keep FixMyCommunity locally focused so you will not be able to report problems outside of the areas we have entered authorities for. The map on your Reports will let you see all the reports you’ve made, wherever they are. If you’re from an authority than the emailed version of the problem report may also contain the closest road or area name on the map.
Coverage area of FixMyCommunity
FixMyCommunity covers the whole of Ghana. No matter where you are, the only thing you ever have to remember is our website address – FixMyCommunity.com. We are currently operating on pilot phase in Cape Coast - Central Region.

Organisation Questions

Who built [% site_name %]?
FixMyCommunity is a project of Node Initiative (an NGO) and is maintained by mySociety. You can donate to help maintain or to promote it.
